HOW ARE IMAGES SELECTED FOR INCLUSION IN THE MEDIA GALLERY?

back to top

Attractive, well-composed, and unique photos of art, events, and ephemera from official community activities are selected from submissions to this gallery. Curators choose beautiful images that document art installations, theme camps, mutant vehicles, performances, compelling playa moments and unique perspectives. We are also seeking exceptional images from official Burning Man year-round gatherings, such as Decompression or Regional Network community events, and representations of civic projects supported by the Black Rock Arts Foundation, Burners Without Borders, and Black Rock Solar.

Note: even if your photos are not selected for the gallery, all submitted images are retained as a valuable part of our digital archives, and we thank you for taking the time and effort to submit your images.

HOW DO I CONTRIBUTE IMAGES TO THE MEDIA GALLERY?

back to top

First, read about the “How are images selected ...” section above.

Second, either login in with your account information from our original image gallery, or register a new account on our media gallery site, and the system will create a contributor account for you. When you log into your account, an “upload” button will be available.

Next to the upload button is another button labeled “My Unsubmitted.” Clicking this button will allow you to see all links to your saved image batches. Click on the batch link that contains the images you'd like to submit. After entering your meta information for each image (title, year, notes, etc.), then simply click the “Save and Submit” button located at the top or bottom of the “Editing Information for Batch ...” detail view. A note will then appear at the top of the page indicating your batch has been submitted to the curation team. Note, there is no way to retract a submitted batch, so be sure you want to commit all images contained within.

Once your images are reviewed, and possibly selected for the public gallery by our curation team, they will then show up in website searches, and also under your name in the Contributors Directory.

As you submit media files, please credit art, mutant vehicles and installation names to their respective artists. Archived artists listings can be found here:

  • Art Installations
  • What is Burning Man

HOW CAN I UPDATE MY IMAGE CAPTION INFORMATION AFTER UPLOADING?

back to top

After logging into your account, click the “my unsubmitted” button to go to a view of your “unsubmitted” batch links (that contain your uploaded images). Clicking on a batch link will take you into a view of all the images it contains and you can utilize the meta fields (title, year, keywords) to update information.

Note, if you've already submitted a batch to the curation team, i.e. clicked the “save and submit” button, then there is no way to update caption information except by emailing mailto:galleries@burningman.com with the corresponding info and a link to the image(s) in question.

WHY IS THE MEDIA GALLERY CURATED?

back to top

We like to think of this gallery and the collection of items as an on-line museum. During the review and selection process, we endeavor to include as much accurate information about the subject matter as possible. In addition to being an official archive for The Burning Man Project, this is also an important communication mechanism to would-be participants, the general public and public agencies. This collection is not intended to be a photo sharing service. Yes, your campmate may be quite adorable while crashed out wearing a tutu after a late night, but we are pretty sure that you can find a few other places on the Internet to show that picture.

WHY DIDN'T MY PHOTO GET SELECTED FOR THE PUBLIC COLLECTION?

back to top

First, read about the "How are images selected ..." section above.

There may be any number of reasons why a photo does not get selected. If your photo is out of focus, covered in dusty playa spots, haphazardly composed, contains explicit adult content, illegal activities, or subject matter not likely to be of interest to the larger community or visiting public, we will most likely not include it. In addition, an effort is made to keep the number of practically identical duplicate mages to a minimum. For example, you may have a lovely shot of The Man burning, but we may already have several similar shots already included in the gallery. It is a good idea to browse the collection and search for similar subject matter before submitting your images.

CAN I ADD VIDEOS TO THE MEDIA GALLERY?

back to top

The Video Gallery will be a hand-picked selection of video offerings about Burning Man and its worldwide community. While we love participant video, plenty of sharing websites exist for such content, so we do not seek to re-create that here. Instead, we'll use it to exhibit curated content from the Burning Man archives as well as works produced by Burning Man or other select filmmakers.

WHY ARE THE IMAGES SCALED SO SMALL?

back to top

In recreating the image gallery, we imported all images from the previous gallery. The former gallery had scaled images to accommodate technical limitations that existed as part of that system. While we have no way to provide larger versions of those images, moving forward, uploaded images should be viewable at a larger size.

WHAT IS THE PRIVACY POLICY WITH REGARDS TO SUBMITTED MEDIA?

back to top

Whenever you submit expressive content to the Media Gallery, Tales from the Playa, or ePlaya, you understand that the submission will possibly be made public on the web site, and that such submission may contain personal information. The Media Gallery includes a link to each photographer and contributor's email address so that interested parties may contact them to request permission to utilize their submissions in other mediums or publications. We are not responsible for the personal information that you or others choose to post in this manner. You may be identified as the author of any posted expressive content unless you request otherwise.
